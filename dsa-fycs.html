Introduction to Digital System Design

Digital System Design is the process of designing electronic systems using digital logic.
It operates on binary values, 0 and 1.
Digital systems process, store, and transmit information efficiently.
Logic gates are the basic building blocks of digital circuits.
It includes both combinational and sequential circuits.
Boolean algebra is used to analyze and simplify digital circuits.
Hardware Description Languages (HDLs) like Verilog and VHDL are used for design.
Digital systems are faster, more reliable, and less affected by noise than analog systems.
They are widely used in computers, smartphones, and embedded systems.
Digital System Design is a fundamental subject in electronics and computer engineering.
